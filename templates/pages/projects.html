Architecture

How the platform goes from ERP data to purchase proposals: the system analyzes forecasting models as the base for planning, a purchase optimizer turns that into buy proposals, and the platform is the base system each client builds on. Agents on top converse and automate, not only answer questions. The whole path is wrapped in each client’s business logic: their rules and constraints shape ingest, models, the purchase optimizer, the platform, and what agents are allowed to do.

From ERP data to purchase proposals

Wrapped in each client’s business logic

→ → →

Select a stage — every step runs inside the client’s business logic.

  1. Business logic. Every stage below is tenant-specific: each client’s rules and constraints wrap ingest, models, the purchase optimizer, the platform, and agent actions.
  2. Ingest. Connectors pull from SAP, Dynamics 365 Business Central, and Sage into S3. Databricks jobs validate the data, apply each client’s rules, and prepare features for forecasting — about 1.5 hours per client.
  3. Forecast. The system analyzes the models — thirteen compete; we keep the best fit per SKU and warehouse. That signal is the base for planning.
  4. Purchase optimizer. Turns the forecast into purchase proposals under each customer’s business constraints — the technical optimization step.
  5. Platform. Results land in PostgreSQL and surface in the Angular app. It is also our base system: clients build their own system on top of it.
  6. Agents. A layer on top of the whole stack: natural-language conversation plus automations — query demand, inspect proposals, and take actions in the platform, always within that client’s business logic.
