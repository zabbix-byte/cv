AI Procurement Platform · Valerdat

The MRP product I designed and built: ERP supply-chain data in, demand forecasts and purchase proposals out — planners can trust. On top, agents don’t just chat: they automate work — querying demand, inspecting proposals, and taking actions in the platform.

  • Re-architected the platform end to end — today operating across 500k+ SKUs and 10TB+ of supply-chain data with Spark, near-instant responses, and demand forecasting at <5% MAPE.
  • Owned the full stack: distributed ETL (Databricks/Spark), a multi-tenant Django backend, and AWS infrastructure, observability, and security.
  • Delivered multi-ERP connectors — Sage 200, Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central — via API/SFTP, listed on the Sage Marketplace.

Architecture

How demand prediction works in the product: ERP data in, purchase proposals out — and agents on top that converse in natural language and run automations, not only answer questions. The whole path is wrapped in each client’s business logic: their rules and constraints shape ingest, forecasting, purchase proposals, the product UI, and what agents are allowed to do.

Select a stage — every step runs inside the client’s business logic.

  1. Business logic. Every stage below is tenant-specific: each client’s rules and constraints wrap ingest, models, purchase optimization, the UI, and agent actions.
  2. Ingest. Connectors pull from SAP, Dynamics 365 Business Central, and Sage into S3. Databricks jobs validate the data, apply each client’s rules, and prepare features for forecasting — about 1.5 hours per client.
  3. Forecast. Thirteen models compete; we keep the best fit per SKU and warehouse instead of one global model for everything.
  4. Purchase. An optimizer turns those forecasts into purchase proposals under each customer’s business constraints.
  5. Product. Results land in PostgreSQL and surface in the Angular app for review and confirmation.
  6. Agents. A layer on top of the whole stack: natural-language conversation plus automations — query demand, inspect proposals, and take actions in the product, always within that client’s business logic.
